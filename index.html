I am a Masters student & graduate research/teaching assistant at Koç University in the Department of Engineering, where I am being advised by Dr. Oznur Ozkasap and Dr. Alptekin Kupçu. I received my B.S. degree in Computer Sciences in 2017 from the School of Science and Engineering (SSE) of Lahore University of Managment Sciences (LUMS), Pakistan, where I worked on Netwrok Security Group as an Undergraduate Research Assistant before moving to Illinois in 2017.

My general research interests lie at the intersection of Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences, such as the areas of Wireless Communications & Networks, Signal Processing, Machine Learning & AI, Cyber Physical Systems, Internet of Things, Mobile and Ubiquitous Computing. Recently, I have been particularly interested in less privacy intrusive and device-free mechanisms (which do not require users to wear any device on their bodies) - other than the typical camera and audio based methods - for Human Computer Interaction (HCI) and Health Monitoring related applications. One of my recent endeavors in this direction was published in ACM MOBICOM and later in IEEE JSAC Human-In-The-Loop Mobile Networks journal: Recognizing small gestures such as keystrokes using Wi-Fi signals. [1-Min Video] [Talk]. The signal processing and feature extraction pipelines proposed in this paper can be leveraged to develop health related applications such as fine-grained sleep monitoring, breath monitoring, etc. using the most the ubiquitous mode of sensing around, i.e. Wi-Fi!
